How Gender Roles Affect Children in Schooling

Why It Matters: Gender roles can heavily impact self-perception, identity, confidence, and social interactions, especially when they are enforced at a highly developmental age.

Portfolio

About

Gender roles can affect children in schooling in a number of ways. For example, traditional gender roles can lead to children being encouraged to pursue certain subjects or careers based on their gender, rather than their interests or abilities. This can limit children's options and make it more difficult for them to achieve their full potential. Additionally, gender roles create stereotypes that affect how children are treated by their teachers and peers, which can lead to discrimination and unequal treatment. This can make it more difficult for children to learn and succeed in school.

I have personally seen these roles exacerbated in the school environment; it wasn't uncommon for my teachers to misgender a peer based on stereotypically feminine/masculine appearance, alienate a gender non-conforming peer, say sexist remarks or jokes, or ask, "are there any strong boys that can help me with X, Y, and Z?"

Further, studies have shown that the enforcing of gender roles can be detrimental to both mental and physical health. Fawcett's Report of the Commission on Gender Stereotypes in Early Childhood (2020) showed that gender roles are linked to higher suicide rates for men, a high prevalance of eating disorders and self-harm in women, and assault of women. The harmful gender roles that cause these effects--in these situations, namely "masculinity" culture and an emphasis on appearance for women--often begin in the home and the classroom.

The consequences of enforcing gender roles in schools are serious: people of all gender identities are being seriously harmed. The normalization and continuation of gender roles is a danger to the mental and physical health of the public. We must break the cycle. While there is no flawless or easy way to do so, there are small steps we can take as a society to work towards acheiving an inclusive school environment that does not push harmful gender roles. Planusa: Gender and Youth Equality

Click here to open link in new window

Gender roles perpetuate inequality and greatly affect minorities that oftentimes lack the power to reject or challenge them. PlanUSA is an organization that advocates girls’ rights, and they partner with adolescent girls, children, and young women to overcome oppression and gender inequality. They help challenge social norms by supporting girls and boys to think differently about gender and the harmful impact of gender norms. They have programs that encourage girls to promote equality and their internal power, and enable boys to self-reflect and identify the detrimental consequences of toxic masculinity. Encouraging young women that they’re just as capable as men helps lower the pay gap, occupational segregation, and denial of promotions to leadership. Moreover it removes the glass ceiling in different professions, decreases the casualization of women workers, and increases the levels of equation and work opportunities given to them. It’s important that we question and deny gender roles and stereotypes in order to create a more equal and fair society.
